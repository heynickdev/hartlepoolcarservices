1. Introduction

Welcome to Hartlepool Car Services. We are committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect the personal data of users of our services, accessible via our website (including at hartlepoolcarservices.com).

This policy has been prepared in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2019.

2. What We Collect

To provide our services effectively, we need to collect certain information. This section breaks down what data we process, where it comes from, and why. We've grouped this into three categories for clarity.

👤Information You Provide

When you create an account using our standard registration form, we collect your Name, Email Address, Phone Number, and a hashed version of your Password. If you contact us directly, we may also receive additional information.

🤝Information from Third Parties

If you register or log in using your Google account, we receive your Name, Email Address, and a unique Google User ID from Google to authenticate you. We never receive your Google password.

💻Information We Collect Automatically

We use essential cookies for security (like oauthstate for Google login) and our servers log basic technical information like your IP address for security and troubleshooting purposes.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect strictly to operate and secure our services for you. Our goals are to be transparent and focused. Here's how your data is used:

  • To create and manage your account
  • To authenticate your identity when you log in
  • To communicate with you about your appointments and services
  • To process your appointment bookings
  • To improve our website and services based on your usage
  • To protect our website and services from abuse
  • To comply with legal obligations

4. Our Legal Basis

Under UK GDPR, we must have a valid legal reason to process your personal data. Our legal bases are:

  • Contract Performance: Processing is necessary to perform our contract with you (e.g., creating your account, managing your appointments).
  • Legal Obligation: We may need to process your data to comply with the law.
  • Legitimate Interests: We may process your data for our legitimate business interests, such as improving our services, securing our systems, and preventing fraud. We only rely on legitimate interests where they do not override your rights and freedoms.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We only share your information in very specific, limited circumstances to operate our service or comply with the law:

  • Service Providers: We may share your data with trusted third-party service providers who help us operate our website and provide our services (e.g., hosting providers, email services). These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data.
  • Legal Compliance: We may disclose your data if required by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., a court or government agency).
  • Business Transfers: If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or a portion of our assets, your personal data may be transferred as part of that transaction.

6. Security & Retention

We take the security of your data seriously. We implement technical and organisational measures to protect it, including hashing all passwords using bcrypt and using secure protocols for data transmission (HTTPS). We will retain your personal data for as long as you maintain an account with us. If you delete your account, we will take steps to delete your information within a reasonable timeframe.

7. Your Data Protection Rights

Under UK data protection law, you have specific rights regarding your personal information. You can click on each right below to learn more about what it means and how you can exercise it. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK's data protection regulator.

You have the right to ask us for copies of your personal information.

You have the right to ask us to rectify information you think is inaccurate or complete information you think is incomplete.

You have the right to ask us to erase your personal information in certain circumstances.

You have the right to ask us to restrict the processing of your information in certain circumstances.

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.

You have the right to ask that we transfer the information you gave us to another organisation, or to you, in certain circumstances.

8. Policy Changes

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the 'Last Updated' date.
